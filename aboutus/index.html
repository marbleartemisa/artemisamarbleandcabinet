50 Years of Stone, Craft & Precision

From a family workshop in Hialeah to a modern luxury manufacturing ecosystem.

Chapter 01
The Beginning
Founded in 1975, Artemisa Marble & Cabinet was born from a vision rooted in craftsmanship, family values, and dedication to quality. Named after Artemisa, Cuba—the hometown of its founder—the company began as a modest cultured marble vanity top workshop in Hialeah, Florida.

Under the leadership of Damian Molina, who serves as president and brings decades of personal experience in the industry, the company made its most significant transformation by breaking the traditional barrier between carpentry and stonework. Historically separated trades were unified into a synchronized production system, allowing Artemisa to evolve from manual cutting and artisanal methods into a highly structured, precision-driven manufacturing operation.
Chapter 02
Evolution
From the 1990s onward, Artemisa expanded into natural stone fabrication and custom cabinetry production. The company transitioned from traditional craftsmanship into digital workflows, introducing CAD design systems and later CNC automation.

This evolution allowed unprecedented precision, scalability, and consistency while preserving the handcrafted quality that defines the brand.
Chapter 03
Technology & Craft
Today, Artemisa integrates European-grade industrial systems such as Breton CNC machinery, SCM Pratix C12 nesting technology, and Olimpic K560 edge banding systems.

This combination of engineering precision and artisanal oversight defines a hybrid production model where technology enhances, rather than replaces, craftsmanship.
